1. Sampling & Shannon's Theorem

Core Concepts

Shannon / Nyquist: To perfectly reconstruct a signal with bandwidth Fmax, you must sample at Fe ‚â• 2Fmax. Violation causes aliasing ‚Äî frequencies fold back and corrupt the spectrum.
Bandpass sampling (undersampling): A bandpass signal centered at fc with bandwidth B can be sampled at Fe = 2B (not 2fc), as long as no aliasing occurs. Valid when: 2fc/n ‚â§ Fe ‚â§ 2(fc+B)/(n‚àí1) for some integer n.

Ideal sampling multiplies by a Dirac comb: xe(t) = xa(t) ¬∑ WTe(t). The spectrum becomes periodic with period Fe: each replica is a copy of Xa(f) shifted by kFe.
